Gesture Library

Learn about the gestures that Boli can recognize. Each gesture is carefully designed to be intuitive and easy to perform.

Basic Gestures

  • Thumbs Up

    Raise your thumb while keeping other fingers closed. Means "Yes" or "Good".

  • Thumbs Down

    Point your thumb downward while keeping other fingers closed. Means "No" or "Bad".

  • Victory

    Raise your index and middle fingers in a V shape. Means "Peace" or "Victory".

  • Pointing

    Extend your index finger while keeping other fingers closed. Means "Hello" or to point at something.

  • Open Palm

    Extend all fingers with palm facing outward. Means "Help" or "Stop".

Numbers

  • One

    Extend your index finger while keeping other fingers closed.

  • Two

    Extend your index and middle fingers while keeping other fingers closed.

  • Three

    Extend your index, middle, and ring fingers while keeping other fingers closed.

  • Four

    Extend all fingers except the thumb.

  • Five

    Extend all fingers including the thumb.

Emotions and Actions

  • Heart

    Form a heart shape with both hands. Means "Love" or "I love you".

  • Wave

    Move your hand side to side with fingers extended. Means "Goodbye" or "Hello".

  • Clap

    Bring both hands together to make a clapping sound. Means "Applaud" or "Good job".

  • Peace

    Form a V shape with your index and middle fingers. Means "Peace" or "Victory".

  • Hang Loose

    Extend your thumb and pinky while keeping other fingers closed. Means "Hang loose" or "Aloha".

Two-Hand Gestures

  • Hello (Two Hands)

    Wave both hands simultaneously. A more enthusiastic greeting.

  • Thank You

    Place one hand over your heart and nod slightly. A sign of gratitude.

  • Please (Two Hands)

    Place both hands together in a praying position. A polite request.

  • Sorry

    Place one hand over your heart and make a circular motion. An apology gesture.

  • Welcome

    Extend both arms outward with palms up. A welcoming gesture.

Tips for Better Recognition

  • Ensure good lighting in the room
  • Keep your hands within the camera's view
  • Make gestures clearly and deliberately
  • Maintain a consistent distance from the camera
  • Practice each gesture to become familiar with it
